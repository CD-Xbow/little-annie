Overview

Little Annie is a virtual polyphonic analog synthesizer that runs in your web browser and features the classic analog components.

  • 2 Oscillators (each with waveform, octave, detune, and level)
  • White noise generator
  • Oscillator mixer
  • 2 LFOs (Low Frequency Oscillators) with flexible routing
  • 4-pole (24dB/octave) lowpass filter with envelope
  • Amplitude envelope (ADSR)

Little Annie has a few additional features courtesy of our digital world:

  • Simple effects: delay and reverb
  • Real-time oscilloscope display
  • Save/load patches
  • MIDI in (Web MIDI)
  • Light/Dark themes

Getting Started

  • Allow your browser to play audio when prompted.
  • Use your mouse or keyboard (see below) to play notes on the on-screen keyboard.
  • Connect a MIDI controller for more expressive playing (see "MIDI" below).
  • Edit parameters using the sliders, knobs, and dropdowns in each module.
  • Watch the oscilloscope to see the output waveform in real time.

Keyboard Controls

  • Click the on-screen keys to play notes.
  • Use your computer keyboard for quick input (Z, X, C... map to white notes; S, D, G, etc. map to black notes, starting from middle C).
  • Press multiple keys for chords (polyphonic).
  • Release the key or mouse to stop the note.
  • Three octaves are available on the on-screen keyboard, from C3 to B5.

Oscillators & Mixer

  • Oscillator 1 & 2:
    • Waveform: Sine, square, triangle, sawtooth
    • Octave: -2 to +2 (relative to played note)
    • Detune: -100 to +100 cents for subtle or extreme detuning
    • Volume: Set level of each oscillator
  • White Noise:
    • Adds a bright, noisy texture for percussion, effects, or thickening
    • Noise Volume: Mixes white noise with oscillators
  • Oscillator Mix:
    • Blend between Oscillator 1 and 2 (0 = only Osc 1, 1 = only Osc 2)

LFOs (Low Frequency Oscillators)

Each LFO can modulate one of the following targets:

  • Oscillator pitch (vibrato or FM)
  • Filter cutoff (sweeps, wobbles)
  • Amplitude (tremolo)
Parameter Description
Waveform Sine, square, triangle, or sawtooth
Rate Speed of modulation, in Hz (0.1–20Hz typical)
Amount How strong the modulation is
Target What parameter is modulated (Osc 1 Pitch, Osc 2 Pitch, Filter Cutoff, Amplitude)
You can use both LFOs at once for complex modulation.

Filter

  • Type:
    • Lowpass (4-pole): Steep 24dB/octave cutoff, classic analog synth sound
    • Highpass/Bandpass (2-pole): 12dB/octave, for brighter or band-limited effects
  • Cutoff: Sets the filter cutoff frequency. Lower for darker, higher for brighter sound.
  • Resonance: Emphasizes frequencies around the cutoff. High values can create a whistling or "squelchy" sound.
  • Filter Envelope: ADSR envelope shapes the cutoff frequency per note.
    • Amount: How much the envelope affects the cutoff
    • A, D, S, R (Attack, Decay, Sustain, Release): Time and level controls for the envelope shape

Amplitude Envelope (ADSR)

  • Controls the loudness shape of each note.
  • Attack: Time to reach full volume
    Decay: Time to reach sustain level
    Sustain: Level held while note is pressed
    Release: Time to fade out after release

Effects

  • Delay:
    • Time: Delay between repeats
    • Feedback: How much delayed signal is fed back
    • Set delay time and feedback for echo effects or subtle thickening
  • Reverb:
    • Amount: Mixes in a simulated room/hall tail for spaciousness
  • Master Volume: Controls the overall output level

Oscilloscope

  • The small display under the synth name shows the output waveform in real time.
  • Use this to visualize your patch and see the effect of modulation, envelopes, or waveform changes.
  • Sharp, spiky shapes indicate percussive or noisy sounds; smooth, periodic shapes indicate pure tones.

MIDI Support

  • Connecting: Click MIDI Connect. If prompted, allow the browser to access your device.
  • Channels: Select the MIDI channel (1–16) to listen for incoming notes.
  • Disconnect: Click Disconnect to stop listening to MIDI.
  • Supported Devices: Any MIDI controller that your browser can access (check your browser's support for the Web MIDI API).
  • Velocity: Notes respond to velocity (where supported by your MIDI device).

Saving and Loading Patches

  • Save: Download your current patch as a .json file.
  • Load: Click Load and select a previously saved patch file to restore all parameters.
  • Rename/Save As: Choose your own filename when saving.

Tips & Troubleshooting

  • If you hear no sound, make sure your audio is not muted and your browser has permission to play audio.
  • Try refreshing the page if audio stops unexpectedly.
  • Some features (like MIDI) may not be available in all browsers. Chrome and Edge are recommended.
  • For best experience, use a desktop/laptop with a physical keyboard and mouse.
  • If you encounter bugs or have feature requests, see the project's GitHub page (below).
